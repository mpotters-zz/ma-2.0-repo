Independent Artist

In 2017, Myles Avery is already on the Spotify US viral 50 Chart (“Cherry Wine” - Overcoats). Now with the upcoming release of his debut EP Weaving, Avery is out to push the boundaries of atmospheric electronic music with soundscapes that envelope as much as they enmesh.

Avery built out his home studio in New York, and worked his way from collaborations with dance companies to production credits with indie-darlings Overcoats and Sorcha Richardson. He landed a weekly residency on Stewart Avenue Radio, and released “Wind Chimes,” a standout on their debut release as a label.

The recipient of multiple commissions, Avery most recently composed and sound designed Transmission - a distributed, augmented-reality performance & installation at the Edinburgh Fringe in 2017. In 2016, Avery closely collaborated with The People Movers on their productions of both Unraveling - a short film - and cl!ck - an interactive performance installation.

Avery’s ambient beats lace through lush soundscapes on compositions so intentional they border on obsessive. His looping and hypnotic rhythms move listeners on a journey that’s as welcome at 11PM as it is at 4AM. These songs are deeply personal trances: the kind you share a solitary dance to on the subway car, the kind that feels like your eyes are closed even when you’re wide awake.
